Fever Causes

Almost any infection can cause a fever, including:
- Bone infections (osteomyelitis), appendicitis, cellulitis, meningitis
- Respiratory infections: colds, flu, sore throat, ear infections, sinusitis, mono, bronchitis, pneumonia, tuberculosis
- Urinary tract infections
- Gastroenteritis (viral or bacterial)

Other causes:
- Low-grade fever after immunizations
- Teething (not usually above 100°F/37.8°C)

Autoimmune/inflammatory causes:
- Rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, ulcerative colitis, Crohn’s disease, vasculitis

Cancers:
- Fever may be the first symptom of Hodgkin disease, non-Hodgkin lymphoma, or leukemia

Other:
- Blood clots (thrombophlebitis)
- Certain medicines (antibiotics, antihistamines, seizure meds)
