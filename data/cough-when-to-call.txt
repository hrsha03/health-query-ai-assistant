Cough 
When to Contact a Medical Professional

Call 911 or emergency services if you have:

- Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
- Hives or swollen face/throat with difficulty swallowing

Contact your provider if you have a cough and:

- Heart disease, swelling in your legs, or worsened cough when lying down
- Exposure to someone with tuberculosis
- Unintentional weight loss or night sweats
- Infant under 3 months old with a cough
- A cough lasting longer than 10 to 14 days
- A cough producing blood
- Fever
- A high-pitched sound (stridor) when breathing
- Thick, foul-smelling, yellowish-green phlegm
- A violent cough that begins suddenly

What to Expect at Your Office Visit

The provider will perform a physical exam and ask about:

- When the cough began
- Sound of the cough
- Patterns or triggers
- Other symptoms (e.g., fever)

They may examine your ears, nose, throat, and chest.

Possible tests include:

- Chest x-ray or CT scan
- Lung function tests
- Blood tests
- Echocardiogram
- GERD evaluation (e.g., barium swallow)
- Viral swabs
- CT scan of sinuses

Treatment depends on the underlying cause.
