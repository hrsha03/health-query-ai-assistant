When to Contact a Medical Professional

Call your local emergency number (e.g., 911) or go to an emergency room if you are dizzy and experience:
- A head injury
- Fever over 101°F (38.3°C), headache, or a very stiff neck
- Seizures
- Trouble keeping fluids down
- Chest pain
- Irregular heart rate (skipped beats)
- Shortness of breath
- Weakness or inability to move an arm or leg
- Changes in vision or speech
- Fainting or loss of alertness for more than a few minutes

Also contact your health care provider if you have:
- Dizziness for the first time
- New or worsening symptoms
- Dizziness after taking medication
- Hearing loss associated with your dizziness
